Thesis Title: Confirmatory Detection and Evaluation of Environmental Stress in Neem (Azadirachta indica)

Supervisor: Dr. B. Karthikeyan

My Master’s research focused on understanding the biochemical and structural response of plant systems under externally induced environmental stress using non-destructive spectroscopic techniques. The study emphasized reliable spectral indicators capable of distinguishing stressed samples from untreated controls and assessing the extent of physiological alteration.

The experimental observations revealed systematic variations in spectral signatures associated with pigments, carbohydrates, lipids, and structural biomolecules, indicating measurable stress-induced modification in plant metabolism and cellular organization. These findings demonstrate the effectiveness of vibrational and optical spectroscopy as rapid diagnostic tools for monitoring plant health and environmental impact.

Experimental Techniques & Instrumentation

  • Raman Spectroscopy: Spectral acquisition, preprocessing, and quantitative interpretation of vibrational fingerprints.
  • FT-IR Spectroscopy: Identification of functional group variations and biochemical composition changes.
  • UV-Vis Spectroscopy: Analysis of pigment-related absorption features and stress-dependent optical response.
